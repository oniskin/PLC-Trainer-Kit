# Introduction.

Now we get to the FUN part!
Wiring the PLC will give you the tactile, nuanced feel of what goes into wiring a control cabinet. With many of the same industrial-grade supplies used in factories and refineries, you will get a small glimpse into the world of electrical tradeswork.

Who knows, you may like it!
From what I hear, the money is not bad. Not bad at all.

# Objective. What you'll learn.

Wire the components of the PLC kit, identifying each component by name, function, use case, and installation best-practice.

By the end, you should have an understanding of the devices listed in the previous section, including:
- Normally Open / Normally Closed Contacts
- Power Supplies
- Control relays (icecube relays)
- motors
- circuit breakers
- Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)
- Input/Output cards
- Terminal Blocks
- Cable Ducks (informally known as Panduit covers, noting Panduit is a registered trademark)

# Guide.

> [!WARNING]
> 24Vdc should be safe to work on, but you should still follow basic electrical safety requirements, including:
> 1. Work when you're healthy (not sick).
> 2. Turn the power to the kit **OFF** at the circuit breaker and remove the power plug before touching wiring/connections.
> 4. Only work with 24vdc and below.
> 5. Inspect your tools. Use tools that are clean and in good condition.

1. Identify the components from the previous section.
2. Wire the kit according to the [Wiring Diagram](../../Wiring%20Diagrams/Kit%20Diagram%20Step-by-step.pdf). This wiring diagram covers each device on their own page, providing a step-by-step wiring guide.
3. Double check your wiring.

More information on wiring the PLC **CPU power supply** connections is on pg. 22 of the [Click Plus PLC CPU manual](https://cdn.automationdirect.com/static/manuals/c2userm/ch3.pdf).

More information on wiring the PLC **CPU inputs and output** connections is on pg. 33 of the [Click Plus PLC CPU manual](https://cdn.automationdirect.com/static/manuals/c2userm/ch3.pdf)
