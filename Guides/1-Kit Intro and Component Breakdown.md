# Why This Kit?
Operational Technology (OT) and Industrial Control Systems (ICS) power the critical infrastructure that keeps our world running, from power grids to manufacturing facilities. For cybersecurity professionals, engineers, and curious learners, understanding how these systems work is essential.

This PLC Trainer Kit gives you a safe, hands-on sandbox to experiment with real programmable logic controllers (PLCs), digital I/O, and industrial wiring—all without the risks of touching production systems. You'll learn how industrial devices communicate, process inputs, and control outputs in the real world.

## The Brain: Click PLC CPU and I/O Card (C2-08DR-6V)
- **Click PLC Plus CPU (C2-01CPU)** - The heart of your trainer kit. This compact programmable logic controller executes your logic programs and makes decisions based on sensor inputs. It's industry-standard, stackable, and perfect for learning real PLC programming.

![Click Plus CPU](https://cdn.automationdirect.com/static/selectors/plcSelector/clickp/C2-01CPU.png)

- **I/O Card (C2-08DR-6V)** - The I/O Card is attached to the CPU. This is the CPU's gateway to the digital world. This card gives your PLC the ability to read inputs (like button presses) and control outputs (like lights and relays). Eight digital I/O points are plenty for learning fundamental control logic.

![Click Plus I/O Card](https://cdn.automationdirect.com/static/selectors/plcSelector/clickp/C2-08DR-6V.png)

## The Input/Output: Red & Green Pushbuttons with Lights (GCX3201-24, GCX3202-24)
Your way of sending commands to the PLC. These industrial-grade pushbuttons (with integrated 24VDC lights) show what a real human-machine interface looks like. They teach you how operators interact with control systems.

## The Infrastructure: 
Factory-realistic wire termination. Instead of breadboards or jumper wires, you'll learn proper industrial wiring practices with screw-terminal connections and jumpers. This is how real systems are built.
- **DIN Rail (DN-R35S1-2)** - The industrial standard for mounting components. You'll screw your PLC, relays, and power distribution onto this rail—just like in real factories. At 2 meters long, you'll have enough to build multiple kits.

<img src="https://cdn.automationdirect.com/images/products/large/l_dnr35s12.jpg" width="200" alt="DIN Rail" />

- **Terminal Blocks** - Professional field wire termination! On the plant floor, this is where field wiring meets the clean control cabinet.

<img src="https://cdn.automationdirect.com/images/products/large/l_knt12gry25.jpg" width="200" alt="Terminal Blocks" />

- **Terminal Block Jumpers** - These tie together terminal block sections for things like distributing our 24VDC to multiple loads.

<img src="https://cdn.automationdirect.com/images/products/large/l_kn10j12.jpg" width="200" alt="Terminal Block Jumpers" />

- **18 AWG Wire (red and black)** - Wire up your kit with quality control cabinet wiring. 18AWG is a little small for use outside a kit or small enclosure, but it is a common wire size found inside control cabinets. Field wiring is usually 14 AWG or 12 AWG for sensors, depending on the run length. If you don't know what any of this means, don't worry! We'll cover the basics in the wiring section.

<img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/51Bkinz+pWL._SX342_SY445_QL70_FMwebp_.jpg" width="200" alt="18AWG Wire, red and black" />

- **5A Circuit Breaker (GMCB-1B-5)** - Your insurance policy. Just like real industrial systems, circuit protection limits damage if we accidentally cross wires. **Safety first!** Bonus: It mounts nicely to our DIN Rail. 😃 

<img src="https://cdn.automationdirect.com/images/products/large/l_gmcb1b5.jpg" width="200" alt="5A Circuit Breaker - DIN Rail Mountable" />

- **24VDC Power Supply** - Industrial systems run on safe, low-voltage DC power. Choose between a simple plug-in adapter for quick learning or a more authentic 120VAC power supply if you're an experienced electrician.

<img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/71gwjBUwwBL._AC_SX466_.jpg" width="200" alt="24VDC Power Supply - Safer option" />
